# Aetna Clinical Policy — Precertification: Arthroscopic Knee Surgery

**Policy Number:** AET-SURG-014
**Payer:** Aetna
**Effective:** January 1, 2026 | **Last reviewed:** March 2026
**Applies to:** Commercial HMO/PPO and Medicare Advantage plans

## Purpose

This policy defines precertification requirements for elective arthroscopic
procedures of the knee performed in any outpatient or ambulatory surgical
setting.

## Precertification requirement

Aetna **requires precertification for CPT 29881** (arthroscopy, knee,
surgical; with meniscectomy, medial or lateral) and for the related
arthroscopic codes 29880, 29877, 29874, and 29882. Claims submitted for
these procedures without an authorization number on file will be denied
with claim adjustment reason code **CARC 197** (precertification/
authorization/notification absent).

Precertification requests must be submitted through the Availity provider
portal or by phone at least **five (5) business days** before the scheduled
date of service. Requests must include:

1. Office notes documenting at least six (6) weeks of conservative therapy
   (physical therapy, NSAIDs, or activity modification), unless the member
   has a locked knee or an acute traumatic tear confirmed on MRI.
2. The MRI report demonstrating a meniscal tear correlating with the
   member's symptoms and physical examination.
3. The planned procedure code(s), servicing provider NPI, and facility.

## Retroactive authorization

Retroactive authorization requests are accepted **only within three (3)
business days** following the date of service, and only when the procedure
was performed urgently or the member's coverage could not be verified at
the time of service. Requests received after this window will be
administratively denied, and the associated claim denial will be upheld on
appeal absent documented extenuating circumstances.

## Claims handling guidance

If an authorization was obtained but the claim was submitted without the
authorization number, the claim may be **corrected and resubmitted** with
the authorization number in the prior authorization field (2300 loop, REF*G1
segment). A new precertification request is not required in this situation,
and the corrected claim will be adjudicated against the existing
authorization on file.

If no authorization exists, providers should not resubmit the claim
unchanged. The appropriate path is a written appeal with clinical records
demonstrating medical necessity and an explanation of why precertification
was not obtained. Peer-to-peer review may be requested within fourteen (14)
calendar days of the initial denial.

## Audit note

Authorization numbers are validated against the servicing NPI, member ID,
procedure code, and a date-of-service window of plus or minus fourteen (14)
days. Mismatches in any of these fields will cause the claim to deny even
when an authorization exists.
