# Cigna Administrative Policy — Coordination of Benefits (COB)

**Policy Number:** CIG-ADM-102
**Payer:** Cigna
**Effective:** January 1, 2026 | **Last reviewed:** January 2026
**Applies to:** Commercial plans

## Policy statement

When a member holds more than one health plan, Cigna coordinates benefits
so that combined payments do not exceed the total allowable expense. Claims
submitted to Cigna as primary when another payer holds primacy — or when
primacy cannot be determined because the member's COB information is stale
— are denied with **CARC 22** (care may be covered by another payer),
commonly with remark code **MA04** (secondary payment cannot be considered
without the identity of the primary payer).

## Order of benefit determination

Primacy is determined in the following order:

1. **Employee/subscriber rule:** the plan covering the person as an
   employee or subscriber pays before a plan covering them as a dependent.
2. **Birthday rule (dependent children):** the plan of the parent whose
   birthday falls earlier in the calendar year is primary. When parents are
   divorced or separated, a court decree assigning responsibility overrides
   the birthday rule.
3. **Active/inactive rule:** a plan covering the person as an active
   employee is primary to a plan covering them as a retiree or COBRA
   continuee.
4. **Medicare:** primacy follows federal MSP rules based on employer size
   and entitlement reason.

## Member COB information

Cigna revalidates COB information every **twelve (12) months**. When the
COB questionnaire on file has lapsed, claims pend or deny under this policy
until the member responds. Providers cannot complete the questionnaire on
the member's behalf, but can accelerate resolution by asking the member to
update COB through the myCigna portal or member services line.

## Resolving a CARC 22 / MA04 denial

1. Verify other coverage at the practice level (eligibility responses,
   registration intake, prior EOBs).
2. If another payer is primary, bill that payer first, then resubmit to
   Cigna as secondary **with the primary EOB attached**. The timely filing
   clock for the secondary claim runs from the primary EOB date under
   policy CIG-ADM-081.
3. If Cigna is confirmed primary and the denial stems from a lapsed
   questionnaire, have the member update COB, then request reprocessing by
   phone or portal — no corrected claim is needed.
4. If no other coverage exists and the member is unreachable, the claim
   remains member liability under most plan documents after sixty (60)
   days' notice.

These denials are rarely resolvable by claim edits alone; they require
either new information from the member or a primary payer's adjudication,
which is why they route to human follow-up rather than automated
resubmission.
