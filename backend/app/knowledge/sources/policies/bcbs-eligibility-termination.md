# Blue Cross Blue Shield Administrative Policy — Eligibility Verification and Coverage Termination

**Policy Number:** BCBS-ELG-019
**Payer:** Blue Cross Blue Shield
**Effective:** January 1, 2026 | **Last reviewed:** February 2026
**Applies to:** All commercial products

## Policy statement

Claims for services rendered outside a member's effective coverage period
are not payable. Services incurred **before the coverage effective date**
are denied with **CARC 26**; services incurred **after coverage
terminated** are denied with **CARC 27** (expenses incurred after coverage
terminated). These denials reflect the member's enrollment record at the
time of adjudication, not a judgment about the service itself.

## Common termination scenarios

- **Employment change:** group coverage typically ends on the last day of
  the month in which employment ends; some groups terminate on the
  separation date itself.
- **Non-payment of premium:** individual-market members receive a grace
  period (thirty days, or ninety days for subsidized exchange members);
  claims during a lapsed grace period pend, then deny if premiums remain
  unpaid.
- **Dependent aging out:** dependent coverage ends at the end of the month
  of the 26th birthday.
- **Retroactive termination:** employers may report terminations up to
  sixty (60) days in arrears, which can reverse previously paid claims.

## Provider verification standard

Eligibility should be verified through the 270/271 transaction or the
Availity portal **within seventy-two (72) hours before each date of
service**. A verification response showing active coverage, retained with
the timestamp, protects the provider in retroactive termination disputes:
when a provider relied on a positive eligibility response and coverage was
later termed retroactively, the provider may invoke the **eligibility
reliance provision** and request claim reprocessing rather than absorbing
the denial.

## Resolving a CARC 27 denial

1. Pull the eligibility history for the member and confirm the termination
   date against the date of service.
2. If the termination date is **after** the date of service, the denial is
   erroneous — request reprocessing with the eligibility response attached.
3. If coverage genuinely termed before the date of service, check for
   replacement coverage (new employer plan, exchange plan, Medicaid);
   registration data or the member can supply the new payer, and the claim
   should be redirected there within that payer's filing limit.
4. If no active coverage existed, the balance is **member liability**.
   Bill the member directly; no appeal to Blue Cross Blue Shield will
   change an accurate enrollment record, so resubmission or appeal of a
   verified CARC 27 denial is not productive.

Members retain the right to dispute their termination date with their
employer or the exchange; providers should direct enrollment disputes to
those channels rather than the claims appeal process.
