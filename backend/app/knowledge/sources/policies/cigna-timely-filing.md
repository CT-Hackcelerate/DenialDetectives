# Cigna Administrative Policy — Timely Filing of Claims

**Policy Number:** CIG-ADM-081
**Payer:** Cigna
**Effective:** January 1, 2026 | **Last reviewed:** January 2026
**Applies to:** Commercial plans (participating and non-participating providers)

## Filing deadline

Participating providers must submit claims within **180 calendar days of
the date of service** (for inpatient claims, 180 days from the date of
discharge). Claims received after the deadline are denied with claim
adjustment reason code **CARC 29** (the time limit for filing has expired),
and the member may not be balance-billed for the denied amount.

When Cigna is the **secondary payer**, the 180-day clock begins on the date
of the primary payer's explanation of benefits rather than the date of
service.

## Corrected claims and resubmissions

A corrected claim must be received within **365 days of the date of
service**, provided the original claim was received within the initial
180-day window. Corrected claims received without an original timely
submission on file are denied under this policy.

## Reconsideration with proof of timely filing

A CARC 29 denial may be overturned only when the provider submits
**acceptable proof of timely filing** with the reconsideration request.
Acceptable proof is limited to:

1. A clearinghouse acceptance report showing the claim was accepted by
   Cigna (not merely transmitted by the provider) within the deadline;
2. A Cigna-generated acknowledgment (277CA) referencing the claim; or
3. For paper claims, a certified mail receipt dated within the deadline.

Screen prints from practice management systems, billing ledgers, and
statements that a claim "was sent" are **not** acceptable proof.
Resubmitting the claim unchanged, or submitting a cover letter without one
of the three documents above, will result in the denial being upheld.

## Exceptions

The filing deadline may be extended in the following circumstances, with
documentation:

- **Retroactive eligibility:** the member's coverage was granted
  retroactively after the date of service; the clock begins on the
  eligibility determination date.
- **Coordination of benefits changes:** primacy was redetermined after
  initial adjudication.
- **Declared disasters:** filing periods are tolled for the duration of a
  federally or state-declared emergency affecting the provider's service
  area.

Provider system migrations, staffing shortages, and vendor errors do not
qualify for an exception.

## Practical guidance for denial management

Before appealing a CARC 29 denial, confirm whether clearinghouse acceptance
evidence exists. If no acceptable proof is available, the correct
disposition is a **write-off to the provider**; the balance cannot be
transferred to the member under the participating provider agreement.
