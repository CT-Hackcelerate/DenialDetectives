# UnitedHealthcare Reimbursement Policy — Significant, Separately Identifiable E/M (Modifier 25)

**Policy Number:** UHC-CP-044
**Payer:** UnitedHealthcare
**Effective:** January 1, 2026 | **Last reviewed:** February 2026
**Applies to:** Commercial and Medicare Advantage products

## Policy statement

An evaluation and management (E/M) service reported on the **same date of
service as a procedure** (including minor procedures with a 0- or 10-day
global period and surgical procedures such as arthroscopy) is considered
included in the procedure's global allowance and is **not separately
reimbursable unless modifier 25 is appended** to the E/M code.

Modifier 25 attests that the E/M was a **significant, separately
identifiable** service, above and beyond the usual preoperative and
postoperative work of the procedure. Common examples include evaluation of
a new complaint at a different anatomic site (for example, a shoulder
evaluation on the day of a knee procedure) or a documented change in the
plan of care unrelated to the procedure performed.

## How these denials appear on remittance

When an E/M is billed without modifier 25 alongside a same-day procedure,
UnitedHealthcare's claim editing system may deny the E/M line with:

- **CARC 97** with RARC M80 (payment included in another service), or
- **CARC 16** with a generic remark code such as **N54**, when the edit
  fires at the front-end validation stage rather than the bundling stage.

Providers should be aware that a CARC 16 "missing information" denial on an
E/M line billed with a same-day procedure is **frequently a modifier issue
rather than a documentation issue**. Before submitting records, verify
whether modifier 25 was present on the E/M line.

## Correction and resubmission

If the medical record supports a significant, separately identifiable E/M:

1. Append modifier 25 to the E/M line.
2. Resubmit as a corrected claim (frequency code 7) referencing the
   original claim number.
3. Do not attach records unless specifically requested; the corrected
   claim will re-adjudicate automatically.

If the E/M reflects only the usual pre-procedure assessment (confirming the
planned procedure, obtaining consent, or a brief focused exam of the
operative site), modifier 25 is **not** appropriate and the E/M line should
be written off.

## Documentation requirements

The medical record must stand alone in demonstrating the separate service:
a distinct history, examination, and medical decision-making for the
unrelated problem. A separate diagnosis code is supportive but neither
required nor by itself sufficient. Records are subject to prepayment and
postpayment review; a pattern of modifier 25 on more than 60 percent of
same-day procedure claims may trigger an audit.
